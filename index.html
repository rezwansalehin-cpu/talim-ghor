The Story

From family home to service

Talim Ghor is described here as a home, a venue, a village center, and a beautiful scenic location in Hatibandha. Its public story is closely tied to KAMPS and the work of Prof. Dr. M. A. Samad, whose vision for community welfare began from this family-rooted place.

Founded by Prof. Dr. M. A. Samad and Nasreen Begum, and maintained by their son Rezwan Salehin, Talim Ghor carries both personal memory and public purpose. It is not presented as a permanent clinic; it is a place where people gather, receive care during annual programs, and remember the language martyrs through service.

Since 2005

A village tradition shaped by care, memory, and public health awareness.

February 21 Camp

Care in memory of the language martyrs

Public sources describe the annual February 21 program as a day of medical consultation, medicine support, laboratory testing during the camp, and kidney-health awareness for underprivileged patients.

Since 2005

A Tangail tradition

KAMPS says Prof. Dr. M. A. Samad began organizing free medical camp activity in Tangail in 2005, and that the program later took formal shape.

Same day, same venue

February 21 at Talim Ghor

The official KAMPS page says the camp is arranged every year on February 21 at the same venue in front of Talim Ghor, Hatibandha in Shakhipur.

Public reports

Thousands reached

News coverage reports over 1,000 patients in 2026, nearly 3,500 in 2025, and 3,000 patients in 2019 receiving free care or health support.

2005

KAMPS public sources trace the Tangail free medical camp activity to this year.

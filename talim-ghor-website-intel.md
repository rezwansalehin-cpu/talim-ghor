# Talim Ghor Website Intel

Working source pack for building a Talim Ghor website.

## Core Identity

Talim Ghor is connected to Hatibandha, Sakhipur/Shakhipur, Tangail. Some public sources use the alternate spelling `Talim Ghar`; the official spelling for this website is `Talim Ghor`.

The strongest public story is:

> Talim Ghor is the village-rooted origin point of a national kidney-awareness and community-health movement, connected to Prof. Dr. M. A. Samad and KAMPS.

## Preferred Positioning

Use Talim Ghor as a place of:

- community service
- rural health awareness
- February 21 remembrance and public care
- KAMPS origin/history
- Prof. Dr. M. A. Samad's village-rooted welfare work

Avoid making it feel like a generic clinic website. It should feel like a meaningful community institution with a living annual health-camp tradition.

## Important Website Claims We Can Support

- Located in Hatibandha, Sakhipur/Shakhipur, Tangail.
- Connected with Prof. Dr. M. A. Samad.
- Connected with KAMPS: Kidney Awareness Monitoring and Prevention Society.
- Annual free medical / kidney and eye camp activity is associated with February 21.
- Public sources say KAMPS began its camp work in Tangail around 2005.
- Public Facebook and web sources describe free treatment/medicine support for poor patients during the camp.

## Publishing Boundaries

- Use official spelling: `Talim Ghor`.
- Describe Talim Ghor as a venue, home, village center, and scenic location.
- Do not publish exact 2026 event time, registration process, or registration fee unless separately requested and verified.
- Do not publish any claim about regular testing, blood testing, dialysis, ongoing clinic service, or permanent medical capacity without separate confirmation.
- Founder wording for this website: founded by Prof. Dr. M. A. Samad and Nasreen Begum; maintained by their son Rezwan Salehin.

## Website Structure Recommendation

1. Home
   - Hero: Talim Ghor, Hatibandha, Tangail
   - Short line: where village memory, health awareness, and community service meet

2. The Story
   - Prof. Dr. M. A. Samad
   - KAMPS origin connection
   - Why Talim Ghor matters

3. February 21 Health Camp
   - Annual free medical / kidney and eye camp tradition
   - Patient care, screening, medicines, awareness
   - Use dates carefully and verify current-year details

4. KAMPS Connection
   - KAMPS mission
   - Kidney awareness and prevention
   - Link to official KAMPS page

5. Gallery / Memories
   - Use local or Facebook-approved images later
   - Organize by year if possible

6. Visit / Contact
   - Hatibandha, Sakhipur, Tangail
   - Directions/map later
   - Contact only after confirmation

## Tone

Warm, respectful, grounded, village-rooted, public-health oriented.

Avoid over-polished corporate language. Avoid aggressive donation or clinic-ad tone.

## Possible Homepage Line

Talim Ghor is a village-rooted place of remembrance and service in Hatibandha, Tangail, where the spirit of community care continues through KAMPS and the annual February 21 health camp tradition.
